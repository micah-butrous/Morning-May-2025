RESILIENCE ACTION INTERNATIONAL.

Resilience Action International (RAI) Overview Resilience Action International (RAI) is a humanitarian and development organization focused on empowering displaced and vulnerable communities, especially youth and women, through education, livelihoods, and leadership development. Founded in 2010, RAI operates mainly in Kakuma Refugee Camp, Kenya, and surrounding host communities.

SERVICES OFFERED AT RESILIENCE ACIION INTERNATIONAL

Key Areas of Focus Education & Training Offers secondary education and bridging programs for out-of-school youth. Partners with institutions like Jesuit Refugee Service and Xavier Project. Focuses on both formal and non-formal education. Youth Empowerment Supports youth-led initiatives. Provides leadership and peacebuilding training. Hosts community dialogues to reduce violence and foster social cohesion. Livelihoods & Entrepreneurship Provides vocational training (e.g., tailoring, mechanics, ICT). Runs income-generating activities for youth and women. Encourages innovation through social entrepreneurship. Peacebuilding & Advocacy Conducts training in conflict resolution and human rights. Advocates for policy changes at local and international levels. Works to bridge gaps between refugee and host communities.
